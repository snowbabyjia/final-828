6.853: First MIT Student Symposium on Computer Systems (SOCS)

The goal of 6.853 is learn to do research in computer systems. 6.853 will involve learning to critique papers/ideas, picking a research topic, building a system/executing a project, analyzing the results, writing it up in a conference-quality paper, and creating an effective presentation. 6.853 is intended for Course 6 Meng students and MS/PhD students.

Method

  • Study, discuss, and critically judge the recent literature of computer systems.
  • Execute a research project in teams of two/three by implementing a cool idea, evaluating it, and writing a 10-page paper on it.

    Topics covered

    Computer architecture, networks, operating systems, programming language/system interface, network protocols, file systems, replication, protection, transactions, database systems, computer graphics, user interfaces, and application systems.

    Project

    Projects are done in teams of maximal 3 students. Suggestions for topics for projects will be provided by the staff, but you are free to make your own proposal for a project. Some of the suggested projects will be executed using the exokernel operating system. Projects may overlap with the research for your thesis (if the thesis is in the area of computer systems and your advisor does not mind).

    Mechanics

    • Class meets twice a week for 1.5 hours:
      • Lecturer gives a general intro to the area
      • Student gives a 20 minute presentation on the selected paper (requires doing additional background reading)
      • The rest of the class meeting is devoted to discussion of the selected paper and project reports.
    • Students are required to write 4 pieces of text:
      • One-page research progress reports
      • A 5-page mock area exam report covering the background of the project
      • A 10-page research papers (see CFP for SOCS).
    • Grading:
      • Participation in class
      • Quality of 20-minute presentation
      • Quality of progress reports
      • Qaulity of mock-area exam paper
      • Quality of paper
      • Quality of final presentation at the symposium

    Prereq

    Highly motivated to do some cool hacking and sufficient background in the areas of 6.004, 6.033, 6.035, and 6.170 (or equivalents thereof)

    Collaboration policy

    Do the right thing. Individual assignments are for an individual and team assignments are for teams.
